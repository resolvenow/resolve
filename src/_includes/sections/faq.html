Frequently Asked Questions

Clear answers to common concerns about loan resolution and support.

01 Which types of loans does Resolve Now assist with?

We provide assistance for various kinds of loans, including personal loans, credit card dues, business loans, and other unsecured borrowings, depending on individual circumstances.

02 How long does the loan resolution process usually take?

The timeline varies based on the lender, loan amount, and case complexity, but most resolutions are achieved within a few weeks to a few months.

03 Will interest or penalty charges continue during the settlement process?

In many cases, we work to minimize additional interest and late fees by engaging with lenders and negotiating fair terms on your behalf.

04 How should I respond to calls from banks or recovery agents?

Once you are connected with us, we guide you on handling communication professionally and, where possible, act as an intermediary to reduce unnecessary pressure.

05 What if a lender initiates legal proceedings against me?

If legal action is initiated, our team helps you understand the situation and connects you with appropriate legal guidance to ensure your rights are protected.

06 Will loan settlement hurt my CIBIL score long-term?

Loan settlement marks your credit report as "settled" instead of "closed," which can initially reduce your CIBIL score. However, with our post-settlement support — including dispute letters and timely lender updates — scores typically recover to pre-settlement levels within 12–18 months, faster than DIY efforts.

07 Do you guarantee a minimum settlement percentage?

We cannot make guarantees due to RBI rules and individual case specifics, but our track record shows significant waivers on principal for NPAs across major lenders. We share real case benchmarks during free consultations so you know expected outcomes upfront — not vague promises.

08 How do you stop recovery agent harassment immediately?

From Day 1, we issue legal notices to agents and banks citing RBI guidelines — including call frequency limits and prohibition on threats — and provide direct access to our lawyers for real-time intervention. This typically reduces unwanted calls significantly within 48 hours of engagement.

09 What if the bank rejects my settlement offer?

Rejections are common when an NPA is under 6 months or hardship documentation is insufficient. We counter with audited financials, hardship affidavits, and RBI-compliant proposals — using bank-specific strategies to turn rejections into approvals, typically within 30–45 days.

10 Can I get a No Objection Certificate (NOC) after settlement?

Yes. Every client receives a signed NOC / No Dues Certificate from the lender, plus a verified digital copy for CIBIL disputes — delivered within 7 days of final payment. We also guide you through uploading it to all credit bureaus (CIBIL, Experian) to prevent future complications.
