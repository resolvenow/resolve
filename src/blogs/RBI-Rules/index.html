RBI Loan Recovery Rules 2026: Borrower Rights Against Harassment

New RBI guidelines aim to stop harassment by recovery agents and protect borrower dignity during loan recovery.

Loan recovery has always been an essential part of banking, but many borrowers have reported harassment through repeated calls, intimidation, and aggressive recovery practices. To address these concerns, the Reserve Bank of India (RBI) introduced stronger loan recovery guidelines designed to protect borrowers while allowing banks to recover legitimate dues.

The new RBI loan recovery rules focus on transparency, professionalism, and ethical recovery practices. These regulations require banks and financial institutions to ensure that their recovery agents treat borrowers respectfully and follow clearly defined conduct rules.

Important: Borrowers have the right to dignity and privacy during loan recovery. Any threatening or abusive recovery behavior can be reported to the bank or RBI.

Key Borrower Rights Under RBI Guidelines

Limited Contact Hours
Recovery agents can only contact borrowers during reasonable daytime hours and must avoid late-night calls or visits.
No Harassment Allowed
Threats, abusive language, intimidation, or repeated aggressive calls are strictly prohibited under RBI rules.
Privacy Protection
Agents should communicate directly with the borrower and avoid contacting neighbors, friends, or employers unnecessarily.
Verified Recovery Agents
Recovery agents must carry identification and authorization from the bank when visiting borrowers.

Bank Responsibility for Recovery Agents

Banks and financial institutions are responsible for the behavior of their recovery agents. If agents violate RBI guidelines or engage in harassment, the lender may face regulatory penalties or investigation. This rule encourages financial institutions to properly train and supervise recovery personnel.

What Borrowers Should Do if Harassed

If a borrower experiences harassment from recovery agents, they should document the incident and immediately report it to the bank's grievance redressal department. If the issue remains unresolved, complaints can also be escalated through RBI's official complaint system.

Allowed Contact Timing for Recovery Agents

Under RBI guidelines, recovery agents are only allowed to contact borrowers during reasonable daytime hours. According to the rules, recovery calls, messages, or visits should generally take place between 8:00 AM and 7:00 PM. Any communication outside these hours may be considered inappropriate or harassing.

This rule ensures borrowers are not disturbed late at night or early in the morning while still allowing banks to communicate regarding loan repayment. If recovery agents repeatedly contact borrowers outside these hours, borrowers may file a complaint with the bank or escalate the matter through RBI’s grievance system.
