PNB One Time Settlement Scheme 2026: Complete Guide

Category: Personal Loan Last updated: 21 July 2026 Reading time: ~11 min

If you've been struggling to repay a Punjab National Bank personal loan and the account has already slipped into Non-Performing Asset (NPA) status, you've probably come across the phrase "PNB OTS Scheme 2026." Before you act on it, it's worth understanding what that phrase actually refers to — because it isn't quite what most search results imply.

Punjab National Bank periodically extends or revises its One Time Settlement (OTS) policy for eligible NPA borrowers, in line with the Reserve Bank of India's framework for compromise settlements and technical write-offs. Rather than assuming a brand-new scheme has launched in 2026, treat this as a continuation of an existing, RBI-governed process — and confirm the exact terms with your branch before relying on anything you read online, including this article.

For borrowers in genuine financial distress, an OTS can be a legitimate way to close an outstanding loan for less than the full amount owed, instead of facing prolonged recovery action or compounding interest. It does, however, leave a mark on your credit report. This guide walks through eligibility, the application process, documentation, and the credit consequences, so you can decide with your eyes open.

What Is the PNB One Time Settlement (OTS) Scheme?

A One Time Settlement is a negotiated agreement in which you pay the bank a lump sum that's lower than your total outstanding dues, and the bank waives the remaining eligible balance and closes the account as "settled." This is different from a regular closure, where every rupee owed is repaid in full.

Banks typically consider OTS only for accounts that have already been classified as NPAs — loans where repayment has been overdue for more than 90 days — and usually only when the borrower can demonstrate genuine hardship: job loss, a medical emergency, business failure, or a sharp, involuntary drop in income. The whole process sits within the boundaries set by the RBI's compromise settlement framework, so PNB isn't free to offer whatever terms it likes on a whim.

Is There a New PNB OTS Scheme in 2026?

Not exactly — and this is the most misunderstood part of the topic. Plenty of pages are titled "2026 scheme" simply because that's what people are searching for this year. What's actually happened is that PNB's existing OTS policy has continued to apply through 2026, rather than being replaced by a distinct new scheme.

Because public-sector banks revise these policies periodically, don't take any third-party summary — including this one — as the final word. Before applying:

  • Check the official PNB schemes page for the current circular.
  • Contact your home branch directly.
  • Ask specifically whether your loan category and NPA date fall within the active policy window.

PNB also runs a dedicated e-OTS portal where some borrowers can check settlement eligibility online — worth a look before you make a branch visit.

Who Is Eligible for PNB's OTS Scheme?

Eligibility isn't automatic. Banks weigh several factors before accepting a settlement proposal.

1. The loan has become an NPA

Most settlement proposals are only considered once the account is already classified as non-performing.

2. Genuine financial hardship

PNB tends to distinguish between borrowers facing real distress — unemployment, medical costs, family emergencies, natural disasters, permanent income loss — and those who could repay but are choosing not to. Supporting documents strengthen your case considerably.

3. Ability to pay a lump sum

OTS exists to give the bank quick recovery. If you can't arrange the negotiated amount within the agreed timeline, the offer typically lapses.

4. Internal policy fit

Approval also depends on the outstanding amount, the nature of the loan, how long it's been an NPA, your recovery history, and which level of internal authority is required to sign off.

Who May Not Qualify?

Settlement gets considerably harder — though not always impossible — in a few situations:

  • Accounts under active fraud investigation
  • Serious wilful-default cases
  • Accounts already in advanced legal or SARFAESI proceedings
  • Borrowers who previously failed to honour settlement conditions

RBI rules do permit banks to frame settlement policies that cover some of these categories, but approval faces much stricter scrutiny, and a settlement does not automatically end any parallel legal or criminal proceedings.

How Much Waiver Can You Actually Get?

This is the biggest misconception around OTS: there is no fixed waiver percentage. The final settlement figure depends on the age of the NPA, outstanding principal, accrued interest, recovery prospects, any available security, and your demonstrated financial condition — case by case.

Some third-party reports cite settlement ranges of roughly 30–70% for unsecured personal loans, but treat that as illustrative context, not a number to negotiate toward.

Factors That Can Improve Your Negotiation

Approval always rests with the bank, but a few habits improve your odds:

  • Submit complete, verifiable hardship documentation.
  • Be transparent about income and other liabilities — banks cross-check this.
  • Show you can pay promptly once an offer is made.
  • Keep communication with recovery officials respectful and consistent.
  • Act on an offer quickly rather than letting it lapse.

Step-by-Step: How to Apply

The exact workflow can vary by branch and loan size, but the general path looks like this.

Step 1 — Confirm eligibility

Speak with your branch or recovery officer to confirm your account is classified as an NPA, that it falls under the current OTS policy, and whether any legal action is already underway.

Step 2 — Submit a written settlement request

If PNB hasn't already offered a settlement, you can proactively apply in writing, explaining the reason for default, your current financial position, and the amount you can realistically arrange.

Step 3 — Submit supporting documents

Job termination letters, salary slips showing reduced income, medical bills, ITRs, business-loss records, and bank statements all help the bank assess your case fairly.

Step 4 — Bank evaluation

The bank reviews outstanding dues, recovery prospects, loan history, and available security. Depending on the amount involved, approval may need sign-off at the branch, circle, or zonal office level.

Step 5 — Receive the settlement offer

If approved, PNB issues a formal proposal specifying the settlement amount, payment deadline, any instalment terms, and the offer's validity period. Read every clause before accepting.

Step 6 — Make the agreed payment

Pay within the specified window — either as a single lump sum or an approved upfront-plus-instalment structure. Missing the deadline can void the offer entirely.

Step 7 — Collect closure documents

Once paid in full, immediately request the No Dues Certificate, settlement closure letter, payment receipt, and loan closure confirmation. Keep them indefinitely.

Step 8 — Verify your credit report

Check your credit report a few weeks later to confirm the account correctly shows as "Settled" rather than still outstanding.

Will a PNB OTS Affect Your Credit Score?

Yes — and this is worth understanding before you commit. Loan settlement is reported differently from a normal closure.

Repay a loan in full and it's marked "Closed" on your credit report. Settle it under OTS and it's marked "Settled" instead — better than an unpaid or written-off account, but still read as a negative signal by future lenders, and it typically stays visible for several years. You can check exactly how your own bureau report treats settled accounts on CIBIL's own site rather than relying on secondhand summaries.

Rebuilding Credit After Settlement

A settlement isn't the end of your credit story. Borrowers commonly rebuild over time by:

  • Paying every future EMI on time, without exception
  • Using a secured credit card responsibly
  • Keeping credit utilisation low
  • Avoiding a flurry of new loan applications
  • Monitoring the credit report regularly for errors
  • Building 12–24 months of consistent repayment history

PNB OTS vs. Loan Restructuring

These two are frequently confused, but they solve different problems.

FeatureRestructuringOne Time Settlement
ObjectiveReduce repayment burdenClose the loan permanently
Outstanding amountFull amount remains payableBank accepts a reduced amount
InterestContinues to accrueSignificant portion may be waived
Credit impactUsually milder if repayments continueReported as "Settled"
Best forTemporary difficultyRepayment that's become genuinely unrealistic

If your finances are likely to recover soon, restructuring generally preserves a stronger credit profile. If full repayment is no longer realistic, OTS offers a defined exit from long-term default.

Before You Sign the Settlement Agreement

Don't rush this. Before accepting any offer:

  • Read every condition, not just the headline settlement figure.
  • Get the final amount confirmed in writing.
  • Ask explicitly whether further interest can still be charged after acceptance.
  • Verify the payment schedule and deadline.
  • Get written confirmation that no further recovery claim follows successful payment.
  • Collect the No Dues Certificate the moment payment clears.
  • Consult a Chartered Accountant on the tax treatment of any waived amount — the Income Tax Department's e-filing portal is the authoritative source if you need to check filing requirements yourself.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does PNB have an OTS scheme in 2026?

PNB has continued offering One Time Settlement under its existing, applicable policy through 2026, rather than launching an entirely new scheme. Confirm the current circular with your branch or the official PNB website before applying.

Who is eligible for PNB's One Time Settlement scheme?

Generally, borrowers whose accounts have become NPAs due to genuine financial hardship. Final approval depends on the loan type, outstanding amount, repayment history, and the bank's assessment of your situation.

How much waiver can I receive?

There's no fixed percentage. The amount depends on NPA age, outstanding principal, accrued interest, recovery prospects, and internal policy. Ranges of 30–70% quoted elsewhere for unsecured loans are illustrative only, not guaranteed.

Will settling my PNB loan affect my CIBIL score?

Yes. A settled account is reported as "Settled" rather than "Closed" — better than a write-off, but still a negative signal to future lenders. A consistent repayment history afterward gradually improves your profile.

What's the difference between settlement and restructuring?

Restructuring changes repayment terms while the loan continues; settlement closes the loan after a reduced lump-sum payment. Restructuring suits temporary difficulty; settlement suits cases where full repayment is no longer realistic.

What documents does a PNB OTS application need?

Typically Aadhaar, PAN, loan account details, the latest loan statement, proof of financial hardship, income documents if available, and a written settlement request.

How long does the settlement process take?

There's no fixed timeline — it depends on document verification, internal approval levels, loan size, and how quickly you respond to requests.

Can wilful defaulters apply for an OTS?

RBI rules allow banks to include some wilful-defaulter or fraud accounts in settlement policies, but these face far stricter scrutiny, and settlement doesn't erase any ongoing criminal or legal proceedings.

Is the waived amount taxable?

It can have tax implications depending on individual circumstances. Consult a Chartered Accountant or check the Income Tax Department's portal before filing your return.

What should I collect after paying the settlement amount?

The No Dues Certificate, settlement letter, payment receipts, and loan closure confirmation — keep all of them in case of any future dispute or reporting error.

Key Takeaways

  • OTS is meant for genuine financial hardship, not a routine discount.
  • The waiver amount is negotiated case by case — there's no fixed percentage.
  • A settled loan is reported as "Settled," not "Closed," and stays visible on your credit report for years.
  • Always get the settlement agreement and No Dues Certificate in writing.
  • Verify the current PNB policy directly before applying — don't rely solely on this or any other summary.

Final Thoughts

A job loss, medical emergency, or business setback can make even a manageable loan feel impossible to repay. If your PNB personal loan has already turned into an NPA and clearing the full outstanding amount isn't realistic, an OTS can offer a structured way out — but it should generally be treated as a last resort, not a shortcut, given its lasting effect on your credit profile.

Compare it against restructuring or a revised repayment plan first, read every clause of any offer, and keep every document the bank gives you.

Where to start: Your home branch, relationship manager, or the recovery officer handling your account is the right first point of contact. They can confirm current eligibility, documentation needs, and next steps under PNB's active OTS policy.
