What Happens If You Don’t Pay a Personal Loan in India?

Understand the consequences of missing personal loan EMIs in India, including credit score damage, recovery calls, legal notices, and loan settlement options.

Personal loans have become extremely common in India. Many people use them for emergencies, medical expenses, education, weddings, or business needs. Since personal loans are unsecured loans, borrowers do not need to provide collateral such as property or gold.

However, financial difficulties such as job loss, business losses, or unexpected expenses can make it difficult to pay EMIs on time. When borrowers miss payments, lenders begin a structured recovery process that can gradually become more serious if the loan remains unpaid.

Important: Missing a personal loan EMI can lead to penalties, credit score damage, and recovery actions. However, borrowers still have rights and options to resolve the situation.

What Happens After Missing a Personal Loan EMI

EMI Payment Reminder
Banks usually start with SMS, email reminders, or phone calls when your EMI payment is missed.
Late Payment Charges
Penalties and additional interest may be added if the EMI remains unpaid for several days.
Credit Score Impact
Missed payments are reported to credit bureaus and can significantly reduce your credit score.
Collection Calls
Banks may increase calls and follow-ups to request payment if multiple EMIs are missed.

Late Payment Charges and Penalties

When a borrower fails to pay an EMI on time, lenders usually apply late payment penalties. These may include late payment fees, penal interest, and applicable taxes such as GST on penalties.

Over time, these additional charges increase the total outstanding loan amount. This is why lenders often advise borrowers to contact them early if they anticipate difficulty in paying their EMIs.

Impact on Your Credit Score

One of the most serious consequences of loan default is damage to your credit score. Banks report repayment behavior to credit bureaus such as CIBIL, Experian, Equifax, and CRIF High Mark.

If an EMI remains unpaid, it is recorded as Days Past Due (DPD) in your credit report. Once the delay reaches 90 days, the loan account is usually classified as a Non-Performing Asset (NPA), which can significantly harm your credit profile.

Recovery Calls and Follow-Ups

If borrowers miss multiple EMI payments, lenders may intensify their recovery efforts. This typically involves regular phone calls, reminder messages, and emails from the bank’s collection department.

While these calls may feel stressful, recovery agents must follow professional conduct guidelines and cannot threaten or harass borrowers.

When the Loan Becomes NPA

If the loan remains unpaid for more than 90 days, the bank may classify the account as a Non-Performing Asset (NPA). At this stage, lenders often escalate the matter to recovery teams, external collection agencies, or legal departments.

The NPA stage usually marks the beginning of more serious recovery actions and a significant drop in the borrower’s credit score.

Recovery Agents May Contact You

Banks may assign recovery agents to contact borrowers and request payment. These agents may call borrowers regularly or sometimes visit their home or workplace.

However, recovery agents must follow strict guidelines. They cannot threaten, abuse, or intimidate borrowers under any circumstances.

Borrower Right: Recovery agents must behave professionally and cannot use threats, intimidation, or abusive language during loan recovery.

Legal Notice from the Bank

If the loan remains unpaid for a long period, the lender may send a legal notice requesting payment of the outstanding amount. This notice generally specifies the total dues and provides a deadline for repayment.

Receiving a legal notice does not necessarily mean court action will immediately follow. In many cases, borrowers resolve the issue through negotiation or settlement before the matter escalates further.

Possible Legal Action

If the borrower continues to ignore repayment requests, lenders may initiate legal proceedings such as civil recovery cases or arbitration processes. These legal steps are typically used as a last resort to recover outstanding dues.

It is important to understand that personal loan default is generally considered a civil matter and does not automatically result in criminal charges.

Loan Settlement Option

If a borrower cannot repay the full loan amount, lenders may offer a loan settlement. Settlement allows borrowers to negotiate with the bank and close the loan by paying a reduced amount.

For example, if the outstanding loan amount is ₹3,00,000, the lender may agree to settle the account for a lower amount depending on the circumstances and the borrower’s financial condition.

While settlement can help resolve debt, it may still impact your credit report. Therefore, it should be considered carefully.

What Borrowers Should Do If They Cannot Pay

If you are struggling to repay your personal loan, ignoring the problem can make the situation worse. Instead, it is better to take proactive steps such as contacting the lender early, requesting EMI restructuring, or negotiating a repayment plan.

In some cases, seeking professional guidance may help borrowers negotiate with banks and find practical solutions to close their outstanding loans.

Quick Summary

  • Missing a personal loan EMI can lead to penalties and credit score damage.
  • After multiple missed payments, banks may initiate recovery calls and reminders.
  • Loans unpaid for 90 days are typically classified as Non-Performing Assets (NPA).
  • Recovery agents must follow professional conduct and cannot harass borrowers.
  • Loan settlement may help borrowers close their debt if repayment becomes impossible.
