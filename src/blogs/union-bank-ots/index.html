What a one-time settlement actually is

A one-time settlement, or OTS, is an arrangement where the bank agrees to close your loan account for less than the full outstanding amount, in exchange for a lump sum paid upfront. It exists because pursuing a small, difficult-to-recover loan through courts and recovery agents for years often costs the bank more than what it would actually collect.

That trade-off is the entire logic of OTS. It isn't a discount scheme and it isn't owed to anyone — the bank agrees to it only when it concludes that a negotiated payment now is worth more than the uncertain prospect of recovering the full amount later. Union Bank makes that call internally on the recovery desk, not through a published annual scheme.

How an account moves from a missed EMI to NPA

Settlement conversations almost never start on the first missed payment. Banks track overdue loans through a sequence of stress categories before an account is formally classified as a Non-Performing Asset, and that classification is usually what opens the door to OTS.

SMA-0 1–30 days

First reminder stage. Resuming EMIs is still the expected outcome.

SMA-1 31–60 days

Follow-ups increase. The bank starts asking why payments stopped.

SMA-2 61–90 days

Recovery notices typically begin here.

NPA 90+ days

Loan is classified as non-performing. Settlement becomes a realistic option.

Most successful OTS applications sit in that last box. A regular, current account almost never qualifies — the bank has no commercial reason to negotiate on a loan it's still collecting normally.

Who tends to qualify

There's no published checklist, but in practice, four things tend to strengthen a case:

  • The account is already an NPA. This is close to a prerequisite rather than a preference.
  • The default has a documented cause — job loss, business closure, a medical event, a family emergency — rather than an unexplained stop in payments.
  • It's an unsecured personal loan. Without collateral to fall back on, settlement is often one of the bank's few practical recovery routes once an account turns bad.
  • You approach the bank before it escalates further, rather than after legal proceedings are already underway.

Accounts involving suspected fraud, wilful default, or an active criminal investigation are treated very differently. The RBI's 2023 framework technically permits settlements in some of these cases too, but they go through far heavier scrutiny and shouldn't be assumed to qualify by default.

One consequence worth planning around: under the RBI framework, borrowers who settle are usually subject to a 12-month cooling-off period before the same lender will consider them for fresh credit. If you'll need to borrow again soon, that's a real cost to weigh against the waiver.

What waiver to realistically expect

This is the number everyone searches for, and the honest answer is that Union Bank publishes no fixed figure. What you're offered depends on the outstanding principal, how much interest and penal charges have piled up, how old the default is, and how recoverable the bank judges your account to be.

0% Reported market range for unsecured personal loans 100%

Practitioners commonly report settlements landing around 30–60% of total dues for suitable unsecured personal loan cases. This is not an official Union Bank figure, and plenty of accounts settle outside this band in either direction.

In practice, negotiations often start with penal interest, late fees, and collection charges — those tend to be the first things the bank is willing to waive. Reductions on the principal itself usually come later in the discussion, and only if the bank's recovery assessment supports it.

Applying for a settlement, step by step

  1. Confirm the account is actually classified as NPA

    Check with your branch, the assigned recovery officer, or any notice you've already received. If the account is still in the SMA stages, the bank will likely push you back toward resuming EMIs rather than negotiating.

  2. Make sure your registered mobile number is live

    Union Bank's Online OTS facility is OTP-based. If your number has changed, update your KYC at the branch first — the portal won't work without it.

  3. Submit the proposal — online or at the branch

    Straightforward personal loan cases can often go through the bank's Online OTS platform. Complex cases, multiple loans, or export/import-linked NPAs are usually better handled in person with the recovery department.

  4. Build a hardship file

    Salary slips, ITRs, medical bills, a termination letter, business loss statements — whatever demonstrates the default came from genuine hardship rather than a choice not to pay.

  5. Offer a number you can actually pay

    An unrealistically low opening offer usually just slows things down. Anchor it to what you, plus family support if relevant, can genuinely raise as a lump sum.

  6. Let the bank review and counter

    Expect a back-and-forth — requests for documents, a revised figure, sometimes a flat rejection. Timelines vary a lot between accounts, so avoid assuming anything until it's in writing.

  7. Read the settlement letter line by line

    Confirm the total amount, the payment deadline, the accepted payment method, and what happens if you miss the date — before you send any money.

  8. Pay only through official banking channels

    NEFT, RTGS, or a branch deposit against your loan account. Never hand cash to a recovery agent, and keep every receipt and reference number.

  9. Collect the No Dues Certificate

    This is the step people forget. Without a formal NOC and No Dues Certificate, you have no clean proof the account is closed — get both before you consider the matter settled.

Documents worth having ready

  • PAN and Aadhaar
  • Loan account statement
  • Recent salary slips or income proof
  • Bank statements (last 6–12 months)
  • Income Tax Returns, where applicable
  • Hardship evidence — medical bills, termination letter, business loss statement

What happens to your CIBIL score

A settled loan doesn't get reported the same way a fully repaid one does, and the distinction matters more than most borrowers expect going in.

Status shownWhat it means
ClosedLoan repaid in full, on the original terms.
SettledBank accepted less than the total contractual dues to close the account.

A "Settled" remark is visible to future lenders on your CIBIL report and can influence how they assess a new application. Some borrowers do ask the bank to revise this to "Closed" after full payment, but there's no guarantee it will be granted — it's entirely at the lender's discretion. The mark's effect on your score does fade with consistent on-time repayment elsewhere, though there's no fixed timeline for that either.

The tax angle people forget

The portion of your loan that gets waived isn't automatically tax-free. Depending on your circumstances and how the waiver is structured, it can carry income tax implications under provisions of the Income Tax Act. Because this varies by individual and by loan type, it's worth a conversation with a Chartered Accountant before you file — ideally before you sign the settlement letter, not after. You can cross-check applicable provisions on the Income Tax Department's e-filing portal.

A few legal points worth knowing

  • Defaulting on a personal loan is generally a civil matter in India, not a criminal one.
  • Cheque-bounce cases fall under separate legal provisions and proceed independently of the settlement talks.
  • Ignoring recovery notices rarely helps your position — responding early tends to keep the conversation constructive.
  • If legal proceedings have already started, get a lawyer to look at the settlement agreement before you sign it.

Mistakes that quietly weaken a settlement case

  • Waiting too long to engage. Silence rarely improves your negotiating position.
  • Opening with an unrealistic number. It slows negotiations rather than speeding them up.
  • Paying on a verbal assurance. Insist on a written settlement letter first, every time.
  • Losing the paperwork. Keep the settlement letter, payment receipts, and No Dues Certificate somewhere safe and findable.
  • Paying through an unofficial channel. Cash to an agent leaves you with no protection if anything is later disputed.

Frequently asked questions

Who is eligible for Union Bank's OTS in 2026?

Mainly borrowers whose accounts are already classified as NPA and who can show genuine financial hardship. The bank also weighs repayment history and how recoverable it judges the loan to be — meeting the basic criteria doesn't guarantee acceptance.

How much waiver can I expect?

There's no official published figure. Market practice for unsecured personal loans often falls between 30% and 60% of total dues, but the actual number depends on your outstanding balance, how old the default is, and the bank's own recovery assessment.

Can I apply for OTS online?

Yes, through Union Bank's Online OTS facility for eligible accounts. More complex cases — multiple loans, or export/import-linked NPAs — are usually better handled directly with the branch or recovery department.

Will a settlement affect my CIBIL score?

Yes. The account is typically reported as "Settled" rather than "Closed," which future lenders can see. The impact tends to ease over time with consistent, on-time repayment elsewhere.

Is the waived amount taxable?

It can be, depending on your situation and applicable provisions of the Income Tax Act. Check with a Chartered Accountant before filing your return.

What happens if I miss the agreed settlement deadline?

The bank can cancel the settlement, reinstate the original outstanding amount, and resume recovery or legal action. If a deadline looks tight, contact the bank before it passes — not after.
